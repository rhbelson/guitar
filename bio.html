About the Artist

Robert Belson began taking lessons at age 7, studying at Levine Music in Rockville, MD with Doug Rogers. In 2013, Robert began taking lessons with Franco Platino in Washington, DC. Since 2013, Robert has performed in venues across the country from Los Angeles and Chicago to New York.


In addition to performances, Robert has played in master classes for artists including David Russell (UK), Jason Vieaux (Cleveland Institute of Music), Manuel Barrueco (Peabody Conservatory), Sharon Isbin (The Julliard School), Oscar Ghiglia (Italy), Thibault Cauvin (France), Petrit Ceku (Croatia), Benjamin Verdery (Yale University), Julian Gray (Peabody Conservatory), Xeufei Yang (China), Eduardo Fernandez (Uruguay), Ekachai Jearakul (Thailand), Álvaro Pierri (Uruguay), Marcin Dylla (Poland), Jorge Caballero (Peru), and Thibaut Garcia (France).

Robert is a 4-time winner of the John and Susie Beatty Music Scholarship Competition and also a first prize winner of the Marlin Engel Instrumental Competition. From 2013-2016, Robert performed solo, chamber, and guitar orchestral works throughout the Washington, DC area at venues including the Kennedy Center, the Netherlands Embassy, Italian Embassy, and the White House.

Robert is currently pursuing his Bachelors of Music at Northwestern University under the tutelage of Professor Anne Waller.
